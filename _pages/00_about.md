I am a computational materials science researcher holding a Master's degree in Physics from IIT Delhi. My research focused on carbon-based 2D materials, particularly graphene and its heterostructures. Using simulations and theoretical models, I analyzed their structural, electronic, and magnetic properties. I also participated in neutrino detector simulation studies at Fermi Lab's Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE), investigating neutrino-matter interactions. I have published seven research articles in leading journals and presented at international conferences. I also enjoy teaching physics and mathematics to high school and undergraduate students.

I am currently applying data analytics and machine learning to address real-world problems. My focus areas include computational physics, mathematical modeling, and applied machine learning. I am proficient in Python, using tools like Scikit-Learn, Numpy, and Pandas. I also have experience with SQL, Microsoft Excel, Microsoft Power BI, and Tableau for data analysis and visualization, gaining insights from complex datasets. I have freelanced on three open-source data science projects with Omdena in healthcare and precision agriculture.

I enjoy tackling challenges and finding practical solutions. I'm currently exploring opportunities to enhance my skills in data analytics and machine learning. My aim is to use this knowledge to make a positive impact on society and contribute to scientific progress.
