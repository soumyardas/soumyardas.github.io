I am a data scientist with over one year of experience in machine learning, data
analysis, and predictive modeling. I focus on developing data-driven solutions
that create positive social impact. My projects have spanned healthcare,
agriculture, climate science, and credit risk modeling, where I used data to
enhance business outcomes.

I earned my Master’s in Physics from IIT Delhi and spent eight years in
computational physics research. I worked on neutrino simulations for the Deep
Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) at Fermilab and later studied
carbon-based materials like graphene. I have since transitioned into data
science and have been working as a Machine Learning Engineer, developing
customized AI solutions.

Key Achievements:
 - Led the deployment of a climate risk model for East African school SMEs, predicting climate-related financial risks using satellite data and machine learning.
 - Developed and deployed high-accuracy machine learning models across multiple volunteer-driven projects in precision agriculture and healthcare.
 - Published 7 research papers that contribute to the field of computational materials science.
 - Won 2 Best Poster Presentation Awards at international conferences.
 - Presented research at global conferences, building connections with other researchers.
 - Taught physics, math, and coding to high school and undergraduate students, improving their learning through engaging methods.

My research background has instilled in me a growth mentality and a commitment to continuous learning. I enjoy tackling challenging problems and supporting teams in reaching their goals.

Core Competencies:

I enjoy working with complex datasets, building machine learning models, and finding insights that drive growth. I am eager to contribute to innovative projects and collaborate with professionals about using data for positive change.
